# Competitor Research Checklist

Work through this before writing any code. The goal is to know whether the problem is real and whether existing solutions are actually weak, before building anything.

- [ ] Find at least 3 existing tools/products that solve this problem today.
- [ ] For each: what do they charge, and what do reviews/complaints say?
- [ ] Search for the problem in at least 2 communities (Reddit, forums, Facebook groups) — is it discussed unprompted, with real complaints?
- [ ] Identify why existing solutions are weak: too expensive, too complex, missing a feature, bad UX, or simply undiscoverable?
- [ ] Write down the single clearest piece of evidence that demand exists (a quote, a review count, a search volume figure).
- [ ] Decide: Build or Kill. If competition is strong and there's no clear differentiation achievable in one work cycle, kill it and move on.
- [ ] Log the decision and evidence in DECISIONS.md, even if the decision is "Kill."
